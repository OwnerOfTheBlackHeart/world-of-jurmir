Duchess Myrine of Du Indos

Picture of Myrine

A middle aged, female gnome, Duchess Myrine has served the royal family since the first king, Pylas of Cuul. When Pylas noticed that his end was nearing and that his son, the now king Pedaeus of Heminus, had fallen to the pleasures of hedonistic Sithrak worship, Pylas placed Myrine in the seat of Duchess of Du Indos with the instructions to keep Indos intact through Pedaeus's reign.

Myrine takes her task seriously and, while she hasn't been able to truly keep Indos in line, she has been able to stay on the positive side of all the other dukes and duchesses. While she hasn't publicly insulted King Pedaeus, she hasn't hidden the fact that she is looking forward to the next king's reign.

Myrine has a patient, and sometimes even aloof, attitude, but she does take her job seriously. She likes to listen to all sides of an issue and find the solution that is the best for both sides. However, these "best solutions" take time and have been a point of friction during times where a rapid response was required. Myrine is aware of this fact, but has done little to address it.

Myrine likes to dress in simple, but fine, clothing. She prefers white and light yellow dresses and underclothes with a green or blue mantle, cloak, or coat (depending on time of year) and the small gold headband that symbolizes the office of the Duke of Du Indos.
