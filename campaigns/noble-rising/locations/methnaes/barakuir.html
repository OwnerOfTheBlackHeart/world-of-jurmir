Located halfway up Mount Neenlin and hidden at the end of a valley formed by the mountains Talaka and Xulllace, is the metropolis of Barakuir. Barakuir is built half inside and half on the surface of Mount Neelin, with a large stone wall protecting the surface portion of Barakuir. Water within Barakuir is drawn from an underground river and magically pumped up into the city, then distributed through a series of stone channels from inside the mountain to outside the walls into the surrounding farms.

The underground portion of Barakuir is primarily home to drow, though dwarves and other underground dwellers make their home within. While not nicer in total when compared to the outside portion of Barakuir, the underground portion has a much harsher divide between the "classes," with slums being carved lower than the higher class residences.

The surface portion of Barakuir is the most densely populated portion of Barakuir, housing markets, apartments, bars, pubs, inns, stables, and everything else needed for a thriving metropolis. The poor, middle class, and wealthy portions of the surface portion of Barakuir blend seamlessly together, with a mostly equal mix of surface races, though humans can have a little more trouble getting settled into Barakuir permanently.

Organizations and Monsters

The Wives of Sithrak

Made up exclusively of female drow, the Wives of Sithrak exist to ensure the worship of Sithrak and to suppress, and destroy, cults to minor gods within Methnaes. While the Wives of Sithrak prefer to use their authority as drow women and priestesses of Sithrak to perform their duties openly, the Wives are more than capable of using subtlety to accomplish their objectives or to gain political power.

While the Wives of Sithrak technically operate all throughout Methnaes, their headquarters in Barakuir is where the highest density of Wives operate and where they posses the greatest influence.

The Wives of Sithrak operate out of Sithrak's Harem.

Titan Towers Productions

Titan Towers Productions is a troupe of bards who perform anything from plays to concerts to dances, all with the added flair of bardic magic. Their ability to use bardic magic to make realistic and fantastic performances, as well as the the low ticket prices, has made Titan Towers Productions highly popular among the populace of Barakuir.

The symbol of Titan Towers Productions is a titan climbing two towers.

Titan Towers Productions is based out of the Titan Towers Productions Headquarters.

Titan Towers Productions is chaotic neutral.

Titan Towers Productions wields both economic and political power, with the ability to build and destroy reputations, plant ideas in the minds of the populace, and other social tricks. For the most part, Titan Towers Productions is more than happy to sustain itself off of "sponsored" performances, however TTP becomes very aggressive when other bard troupes attempt to setup shop within Barakuir, either driving them out or absorbing them into Titan Towers Productions. TTP also doesn't tolerate particularly skilled professional performers that aren't a part of their troupe.

Notable Locations

Outer Barakuir

Titan Towers Productions Headquarters

The headquarters of Titan Towers Productions is that of a great, cathedral-like building with a massive amphitheatre with carefully tuned acoustics, rentable meeting rooms, a practice stage, acoustically tuned practice rooms, and offices. The building itself is made with a mix of marble, sun dried brick, concrete, brass and wood, all carefully placed to give a sense of awe and wealth. The headquarters is located at the meeting point of the surface and underground sections of Barakuir, allowing the light-sensitive citizens to comfortably catch daytime performances thanks to a shaded walkway from the exit to the surface.

The headquarters of Titan Towers Productions.

Rynrin's Mansion

Rynrin's Mansion is a large, 3 story building, in the surface portion of Barakuir, made of dwarven carved stone with a wooden extension on either side and surrounded by a decorative wrought iron fence. The central section of the mansion serves as the personal residence of Rynrin, with servants' quarters, sitting rooms, her office, and personal kitchen all located within. The western (left) addition contains reasonably nice, hotel-like rooms with a bed, small desk, sitting chair and chamber pot each that are offered to the homeless and recently dispossessed. The eastern (right) addition contains a large eating area and an equally large kitchen which serves as a local soup kitchen run partially by Rynrin's staff and partially by the local church of Durn.

Rynrin's Mansion serves as Rynrin's home and her base of operation for her various lobbying efforts and charitable initiatives.

Inner Barakuir

Castle Ichiir

Seated at the highest tier of the underground portion of Barakuir, Castle Ichiir is a 7 story tall castle carved from stone with defensive walls and towers that protect both the castle itself and its 2 baileys. Within the baileys are the housing and smithies that support Castle Ichiir's staff and guards, while the keep of the castle is surrounded by a large, decorative mushroom garden and a food garden.

The inside of Castle Ichiir is kept immaculate and has themed rooms throughout the castle, 12 sitting rooms, a great hall, a ballroom, an arcane lab, seemingly countless decorations, and a gallery containing art, and other decorations, that don't have a place in any of the themed rooms. Servants and slaves scurry about Castle Ichiir seemly constantly, going about their various duties.

The Pit Market

Taking up the lowest tier of Barakuir's underground portion, the Pit Market is the "drows' market" where just about any mundane vice can be purchased, from drugs and poisons to whores and slaves. The market itself is made primarily of light wooden booths and cart-shops that can be quickly and easily moved as merchants and traders come and go, all centered around the slave market itself, which is a circle of cage-carts and slave merchants surrounding a large stage made from wood-like mushroom planks that serves as a display for slaves on auction as well as the gallows when called for.

Drow and duergars make up most of the visitors to the Pit Market, as the other residents would rather not risk becoming goods. A surprising number of well dressed, and thoroughly guarded, individuals visit the market, despite the need to pass through the slums of Barakuir to reach the Pit Market.

The Pit Market is owned and managed by Valatar Hun'ath.
