Organizations and Monsters

Teamsters' and Porters' Economic Alliance of Taaira Ruzara

The Teamsters' and Porters' Economic Alliance of Taaira Ruzara is an organization made up of several guilds across Taaira Ruzara that cover porters and teamsters who handles collecting and distributing transportation jobs, both singular and recurring. The Alliance also serves to ensure that trade routes and roads are maintained and protected, though the guild does not offer any mercenary services to help in this task. For protection, the Alliance typically relies on local guards and slayers.

The Teamsters' and Porters' Economic Alliance of Taaira Ruzara is actually interested in protecting its members.

The Slayers' Guild

The Slayers' Guild is an organization responsible for managing a social caste known as "slayers" who are hired to kill monsters, criminals, and enemies of the state or retrieve objects from monsters and/or dungeons. The Slayers' Guild is the largest source of jobs for slayers in Keldhall, but has been suffering from a reduction of slayers as of late. It uses the same ranking system as the Slayers' Guild of Mensharra.

The two most common jobs for slayers in Keldhall are clearing out areas of the forests of animals and hunting down bandits.

Woodcutters' Guild

The Woodcutters' Guild is an organization responsible for managing lumberers and for ensuring that the forests surrounding Keldhall remain in a sustainable state. The Woodcutters' Guild is also in charge of ensuring the quality of tools for woodcutting as well as the safety of woodcutting, allowing the Woodcutters' Guild to cut smiths off from lucrative jobs making/maintaining axes and saws, as well as cut woodcutters off from tools, jobs, and buyers.

The symbol of the Woodcutters' Guild is a two person saw over a crossed axe and hammer.

The Woodcutters' Guild is more than willing to abuse the economic power that the guild wields.

Shipbuilders' Guild

The Shipbuilders' Guild is an organization responsible for managing the Keldhall's dry dock as well as handling the training of shipbuilders.

Mercers' Guild

The Mercers' Guild is an organization responsible for managing mercers, or textile merchants. The Mercers Guild offers training on textiles, rentable storefronts, and buyer/seller matching. The guild maintains a reliable stock of textiles that they will only sell to their members.

The Mercers' Guild mostly sells to the large number of travelling merchants and the dwarves of Mam Daruhm.

The Mayoral Council

The Mayoral Council is a group in charge of electing the mayor of Keldhall every 10 years. The Council is made up of 5 members that are elected by the merchant class that can acquire one of the 101 voter's licenses every 5 years. While the mayor handles day to day administrative duties and the drafting of laws, the Mayoral Council is in charge of accepting or denying any laws and budgets the mayor presents.

The Mayoral Council does not choose who is running for mayor, only which of the final 2 candidates becomes mayor. When time for a new major rolls around, the mayor selects 3 candidates (possibly including themselves) and the town guilds may elect up to 7 candidates, which the 101 voters then vote on, with the 2 most popular going to the Mayoral Council for the final vote.

The Mayoral Council is elected via the local guilds, with a spot reserved for a representative of the local duke.

Notable Locations

The Hall of Guilds

The Hall of Guilds is a massive 3 story, waddle and daub building painted yellow and green that serves as the administrative hub of all the legal guilds within Keldhall. The first floor is a hall of offices for the basic representatives of the various guilds that can handle joining, leaving, and lodging complaints with the guild as well as a basic kitchen and eating area at the back of the first floor. The second floor holds 8 large rooms in which basic training, meetings and lectures can be held. The third floor holds the more advanced administrative offices of the guilds where guild members can go to handle being assigned jobs and the like.

A basement with a shared vault handles the distribution of rewards for completed tasks. A writ of completion and the job receipt are required to retrieve anything from the vault.

Town Hall

A longhouse style log building with plenty of windows, the town hall hosts administrative offices, including the office of the mayor and the court of the town judge. The workers of the town hall can help with many basic functions including, but not limited to, registering a new guild, paying taxes, or notarizing deeds.

Riverside Supply

The first shop one sees upon entering Keldhall from the south, Riverside Supply is a 2 story tall wattle and daub building with light blue painted plaster and a set of shuttered windows. The front door of Riverside Supply is located under an overhung second floor, with a iron sign of a stylized river with "Riverside Supply" stamped through it. The inside of the store is a small room with 2 tables and 12 chairs, with a barred counter at the back, behind which shelves of goods are displayed.

Riverside supply is a general goods store, but heavily stocks lumbering tools, rope and dried foods.

Riverside Supply is owned by Thessana Screaming River.

Frygam's Axe

Frygam's Axe is a long, log building with a stone front face that holds the forge that Buddy typically works. A dried mud roof covers the forge itself. Hanging out from a silver rod placed at the edge of the mud roof is a silver sign of a dragon's head with the axe still in it.

Frygam's Axe is owned by Buddy Pendragon.

Frygam is the name of a dragon that Buddy and Arthur killed together.

The House of the Hunter

Sitting just outside the western wall of Keldhall is a small cave made from 9 trees that have grown together into a single building. Inside of the cave is a small kitchen, a bed, a set of drawers, an alchemy lab, and a table with enough room to seat 12. Next to the cave is a small path that leads to a pile of woven sticks and shrubs with seasonal coverings and a small shrine to Hrefna, made from done and draped with deer hide. Just off of the path, and directly behind the cave, is a herb garden that always has something growing in it.

The House of the Hunter is operated by Huntsleader Nare T'ael.

Goldenglow Bar and Bed

A massive wattle and daub building with two stories, Goldenglow Bar and Bed always seems to be active with a warm golden glow erupting from it. Inside the building is a large room filled with tables and chairs, with 20 hammocks, with average lock chests, lining the walls, a set of stairs to the upper floor and a long bar. The upper story includes 10 rentable single person rooms, 5 rentable family rooms (which can comfortably fit 4 each), a "caravan suite" that can comfortably fit 15, and a "merchant's suite" that comfortably fits 10, but only has (high quality) amenities for 1.

Goldenglow is owned by Isaac Khan.
