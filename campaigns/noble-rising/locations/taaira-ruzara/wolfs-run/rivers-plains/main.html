Resources

Goods: Goods represent physical items necessary for an activity, which can be permanent fixtures or consumable items. For building an inn, Goods are the materials used to build the structure, the tables and chairs, and the food and beverages you plan to sell. Goods as capital are an abstraction so that you don’t need to keep track of gathering things like stones for a building’s foundation, timber for the walls, ingredients for the menu, and so on. Goods might also represent natural resources (such as fertile soil or a spring), in which case you’re not literally moving these items to a specific location — instead, you’re spending capital to acquire a location with those resources.

Food: A special subvariety of Goods, Food represents the raw food that feeds a community. Excess food is automatically converted to goods at a rate of 5 Food to 1 Good. 1 unit of Food feeds 10 population, rounding up. If you don't have enough Food income to feed your population generated, you lose 1 Goods per 10 population, rounding up.

Influence: Influence represents your ability to get other people in the settlement to perform favors for you or use their skills to accomplish things (as opposed to Labor, which involves hard physical work). This includes getting a merchant to change the terms of a contract, or convincing a politician to do you a favor.

Labor: Labor represents using workers to accomplish tasks. This includes hiring carpenters to construct a building, hiring thugs to extort shopkeepers, using assistants to help you craft items or tend injuries, or hiring employees to run your business while you’re away. For every 10 population that isn't working, 1 Labor is generated.

Magic: Magic represents magical power at your disposal. Some activities, such as healing sick peasants in the slums or constructing a magical library, specifically require you to spend Magic.

Purchasing Resources

Resources can be purchased with gold, which is more expensive than building up the resources over time. When purchasing resources, you gain the resources immediately. This may represent paying carpenters from the next settlement over to help bolster your own carpenters, or it might represent buying the wood from another barony.

The prices listed in the table above represent the cost of buying one unit of resources. Excess Goods, Labor and Magic can be sold for profit, at a return of half their purchase cost.

Earning Resources

Resources can be passively earned over time by buildings or improvements that are manned, once per month. Buildings that are not manned do not produce resources.

Population

Combatants range in level from 1st to 12th, with minors being 1st to 3rd, mediums being 4th to 6th, majors being 7th to 9th, and heroics being 10th to 12th. Within each combatant category, there is 5 lower level combatants for each level higher. For example, if you have 12 Combatant, minors, then you have 10 at level 1 and 2 at level 2. You would get your first level 3 once you have 31 combatant, minors, which would be 25 at level 1, 5 at level 2, and 1 at level 3.

Buildings

Small House

A small house with 1 to 3 separate rooms, typically just a shared bedroom, kitchen, and storage room or a single, all purpose room.

Effect: A small house can house up to 3 untrained, minor combatant, and/or divine population.

Cost: 2 labor, 3 goods

Large House

A medium sized house with 4 to 10 rooms, typically with multiple separate bedrooms, a kitchen, storage room and a social room.

Effect: A large house can house up to 4 untrained, trained, combatant (minor and medium), and/or divine population.

Cost: 5 labor, 8 goods

Barracks

A free standing barracks is a medium sized building dedicated to housing and feeding military types. They typically the sleeping area (barracks), an external kitchen, and a bar area where the solders eat and socialize. Versions that are attached to other buildings are just the sleeping area (barracks).

Effect: A barracks can house up to 15 combatant (minor and medium) and/or divine population.

Cost: 5 labor, 8 goods

Quality Home

A medium sized home with a focus on comfort for a small number of people. These homes often have a bedroom for the parents, a bedroom for the grandparents (or two if necessary), a bedroom for the children, and a guest bedroom, as well as both an indoor and outdoor kitchen, a storage room, an ice room or larder, a den or office, a living room, and a social room.

Effect: A quality home can house up to 2 untrained, trained, combatant (minor, medium, and major), divine, and/or arcane population.

Cost: 10 labor, 12 goods

Mansion

A mansion is a large, non-fortified home intended to serve as the residence of important, wealthy or powerful members of a settlement. A mansion is typically 2 to 3 stories tall with 10 to 20 rooms. This often includes a library/office combo, a dance hall, a social room, a living room, a large interior kitchen, a personal armory (if appropriate), and servants' bedrooms.

Effect: A mansion can house up to 2 trained, arcane, divine, or combatant (major and heroic) population as well as up to 5 combatant (minor and medium) and 10 untrained population. In order to house an arcane, combatant (major) or combatant (heroic), the 10 untrained population housed in the mansion work in the mansion.

If a mansion is worked by the 10 untrained population, then each of the trained, arcane, divine, or combatant (major and heroic) population living in the mansion generates 1 influence.

Cost: 50 labor, 150 goods

Small Farm

A small farm is the size of farm that would typically be worked by a family with at 3 working bodies (typically a father and 2 sons, or a father, mother, and son). This assumes that the 3 persons are given as much land as they can work, rather than as much as they need to feed themselves.

Effect: When worked by 3 untrained population a small farm produces 1 food.

Cost: 1 labor, 1 goods

Large Farm

A large farm is typical of a lord's private fields, taking multiple families to operate.

Effect: When worked by 10 untrained workers a large farm produces 4 food.

Cost: 10 labor, 5 goods
