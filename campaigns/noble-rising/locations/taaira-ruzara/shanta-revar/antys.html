GM Notes

The highest leader of Antys is the Arch Mage of Antys (Baelihareth) who handles immediate problems as they come up. While the Arch Mage has executive power, legislative power is held by the Council of Knowledge and Wisdom, of which the Arch Mage serves as the chairman. The Council is made up of the Arch Mage, the head mage of each of the eight schools of magic (lvl 10), the high priest (lvl 10) from each of the 5 elven churches, and the 4 elder druids (lvl 10).

The lower levels of Antys are available to all who remain civil within Antys, however the upper levels are only available to elves and those who have direct elven ancestry that is willing to testify that they are elven (such as a parent or grandparent). Other exceptions may be applied for, in which case the non-elf is given a pass to be allowed to enter a specific section of the upper trees.

Organizations and Monsters

The Holy College of the Deciphered Mystery

The Holy College of the Deciphered Mystery is the school of the arcane that serves as one of the largest attractions in Antys for non-elves. The school's day to day operations are managed by the worshippers of Kriotz, while the large decisions are either brought to the Council of Knowledge and Wisdom or, if they pertain to classes or less than 3 schools of magic, are made by the appropriate head mages.

Students and instructors of the Holy College of the Deciphered Mystery wear robes whose color is determined by their specialization: purple (Abjuration), yellow (Conjuration), white (Divination), gold (Enchantment), red (Evocation), brown (Illusion), black (Necromancy), blue (Transmutation), and gray (Universalist).

The Council of 7

The Council of 7 is an organized crime ring (often referred to as a guild) that operates within the elven glades that functions as 7 different "sections," each with its own member on the Council. The 7 sections of the Council of Seven are theft, fencing (buying/selling stolen goods), drugs, force (collecting protection money), transport (of illegal or stolen goods), political (bribery and the like), and spying. For the most part, the 7 sections of the council act independently, communicating with other sections when it would benefit them.

Notable Locations

The Holy College of the Deciphered Mystery

The Holy College of the Deciphered Mystery is the largest great tree Throbrar (sage crafter), 150 levels, within Antys and the great tree which stands in the center of the grove. The first 20 levels are dedicated to servicing non-mages and new mage onboarding while the next 10 levels are dedicated to the church of Kriotz, the next 30 floors are dedicated to living space and shopping for the students and staff, and the top 15 floors are dedicated to the government of Antys with the rest of the levels being laboratories, classrooms, and libraries of the college.

Yonna works the front desk as secretary. The Holy College of the Deciphered Mystery is the only great tree in which anyone is allowed to access every level of the tree.

When within the Holy College of the Deciphered Mystery, all wizards are required to wear the robes appropriate to their specialization. If a wizard is found to be without college robes, or wearing the wrong school's robes, their spellbook is burned and they are imprisoned for two weeks.

The Hall of Wisdom

Located on the 10th level of the great tree Veraith (peaceful home), the Hall of Wisdom is a massive church grown into the trunk of the great tree. It is made up of two stories, the first which is for public worship while the second is the living spaces of the clergy.

The entrance to the Hall of Wisdom is an open room with a 12 foot tall ceiling and walls covered in murals of the 5 elven gods convening, of early elven civilization, and of Durn guiding mortals that has a gold carpet going down the middle and pews, desks, and tables and chairs along the sides of the room. At the end of the entrance is a set of large wooden doors that depict Kyranious creating Kriotz, Durn, and Sithrak that leads to a chapel that seats 500 and is designed so that the speaker can be easily heard from the altar.

On the right wall of the entrance hall are doors to offices that offer healing services, emotional and spiritual services, and government services as well as doors to the upper story of the Hall of Wisdom. On the left wall are doors that lead to the Hall's library, kitchens, dining rooms, and classrooms.

The Hall of Wisdom is a temple dedicated to Durn, and his wisdom, that is lead by the priestess sister Verabrar.

The Hall of Agility

Located on the 1st level of the great tree Kethean (wind rider), the Hall of Agility is a 3 story structure grown into the root of the great tree. The front of the building has a set of stairs that leads to the 3rd story, where the messenger and courier service maintained by the clergy of Feph is located. The ground floor entrance to the Hall leads into the shrine to Feph.

The shrine on the ground floor of the Hall of Agility is made up of six separate round stone shrines, each of which is accessible by the central hall. The central hall is left to its natural woody color with carvings of wolves and eagles and even a mural carving of Feph fighting with Durn. The ground floor has a pack of wolves that lounge within it.

The top floor has a large, wooden counter behind which various couriers will work. Behind the desk is a collection of cabinets, desks, chests, and mail bins that hold the mail and packages that the Hall of Agility is entrusted with.

The Hall of Agility is a temple dedicated to Feph, and his agility, that is lead by the priestess sister Arilys.

When Sariel enters the Hall of Agility, one of the wolves lounging about will look to her and say "Walk with the wind and live with the land, for today's cheer may be tomorrow's blade." The wolf is normal after this statement.

The clergy of Kriotz that serve at the Hall of Knowledge serve to run the day to day operations of the Holy College of the Deciphered Mystery and, occasionally, serve as teacher at the College.

The Hall of Charisma

Located on the 18th level of both the great trees Throbrar and Kaothi, the Hall of Charisma is a long, single story building built on the point at which a branch of the great Throbrar winds itself around a branch of the great tree Kaothi. Even by elven standards the Hall of Charisma is a work of art with stories carved and painted into every square inch of the building with large glass artworks making up the windows of the Hall and wooden statues carved into the load bearing columns of the building. Despite being only a single story tall, the Hall of Charisma has 4 distinct sections: The Library of Works, the Gallery of Works Recent and Great, the practice rooms, and the Bar of Liquid Joy.

Closest to the great tree Throbrar is the is the Gallery of Works Recent and Great, a large room where local statues, jewelry, paintings, written songs and poems, artistically made clothes, and more are displayed. The hard to steal pieces, such as large statues and paintings within locked cases, are kept at the front entrance of the Gallery. In the back, surrounded by the easily stolen works, is an alter to Lindo that is topped with a 3 foot tall, painted iron statue of pre-lich Lindo. The Gallery is "patrolled" by various clergy of Lindo who keep it clean, answer questions, and work to prevent theft.

Attached to the Gallery of Works Recent and Great is the Bar of Liquid Joy, which is a long room with three stages, plenty of round tables, and a bar that runs the length of the room. The bar usually has at least 3 bartenders. Behind the bar is a door to the kitchen, which usually has two to four kitchen workers. The Bar of Liquid Joy is often packed with bards, locals, and wizards and it is a rare sight to see a meal time without all three stages in use.

Attached to the Bar of Liquid Joy is the collection of mostly empty rooms known as the practice rooms. These rooms are open rooms with closets and cabinets filled with supplies ranging from paint to ink to clay with easels and ladders and stands. Many of the practice rooms are left with multiple, partially completed works while the artists rest and sometimes they will be used for classes offered by the worshippers of Lindo. When a room will be used for one, or more, classes the room will be marked as such.

The final section of the Hall of Charisma, and the closest to the great tree Kaothi, is the Library of Works. The library is broken up into a main room and a loft, with the loft containing smaller works while the ground floor contains many large works. Within both the main room and the loft are works as varied as those in the Gallery, and most within the library have spent time within the Gallery. Randomly spread throughout the displays are eight person, square, wooden tables with matching chairs at which those studying, or just generally enjoying, the works within the Library may be found.

The Hall of Charisma is a temple dedicated to Lindo, and his artistry, that is lead by the priestess sister Arispar Lyath.
