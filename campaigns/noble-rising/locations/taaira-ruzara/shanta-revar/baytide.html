GM Notes

While the town as 6 full time guards, most of the actual protection of the village is done by the 50, or so, mercenaries that make their living protecting the wealthy and their property.

The Short River, which flows from Crystal Lake, is the the river that Baytide is built on the edge of.

Organizations and Monsters

Black Eye Jack's Redistributers

A group of fences that operate out of Black Eye Jack's pub, giving a cut of the profit from the pirates to Black Eye Jack. They may work in the corners of this pub or in his wine cellar depending on how much money they make Jack and how much he trusts them.

The Eyes of the King

The Eyes of the King is a small Red King cult operating out of Baytide, which currently has only four members. They meet inside a cave hidden on Timothy Foster's farm, which is adorned with Red King iconography.

Their priest Brayden Baxter, a 9th level cleric, leads the cult. The Eyes of the king are also preparing for a Red Lady to be born from Kaitlyn Evans.

Teamsters' and Porters' Economic Alliance of Taaira Ruzara

The Teamsters' and Porters' Economic Alliance of Taaira Ruzara is an organization made up of several guilds across Taaira Ruzara that cover porters and teamsters who handles collecting and distributing transportation jobs, both singular and recurring. The Alliance also serves to ensure that trade routes and roads are maintained and protected, though the guild does not offer any mercenary services to help in this task. For protection, the Alliance typically relies on local mercenaries.

The Teamsters' and Porters' Economic Alliance of Taaira Ruzara is based out of Desserr. Rosalie Newman is the Administrator for the Baytide branch.

The Teamsters' and Porters' Economic Alliance of Taaira Ruzara is actually interested in protecting its members.

Notable Locations

Jack's Pub

A large, single room pub built next to a warehouse, Jack's pub is run by Black Eye Jack. The pub has two fireplaces, one on the north side and one on the south side, that keep the building warm. The bar runs across half of the east wall, with a door to the kitchen behind it. To the south of the bar is 3 doors that lead to private planning rooms. The rest of the main room is tables and chairs.

Jack's Pub is only open from sunset to sunrise and is worked by Jack and whichever kitchen worker he decides to call upon for the day. Jack's Pub doesn't have rooms for rent.

Jack's Pub serves boaters and pirates alike, as well as many questionable people.

The Temple of the Unfinished God

The Temple of the Unfinished God is made of dwarven stone with masterfully made mosaic glass. The main room of the temple is big enough to handle 100 people in the pews with excellent acoustics. Behind the stage stands a 20 foot tall mithral and copper statue of Zigies. On the side walls are doors to the monasteries that house the monks and nuns that maintain the temple and work the forges behind the temple.

The current priest of Zigies is Brother Bromnar Forgeborn.

The Wolf Riders Mail Service

The Wolf Riders Mail Service is a relatively simple wattle and daub building with two stories and a surprisingly large set of stables behind the building. The main room consists of a desk which handles setting up and retrieving deliveries as well as handing mail off to the couriers. Behind it is several locked cabinets filled with mail ready for pickup and an entrance to the mail vault in the basement. The publicly accessible upper story is a temple to Feph with a 2 foot tall, painted wooden monument to him.

The current priest of Feph is Brother Fissuss Jumping Fire.

Driftwood Shipsmith

Driftwood Shipsmith takes up the north most stone dock in Baytide and is actually a dry dock with a storefront and 2 warehouses. The storefront is small, wood plank shack with shelves that have miniature river boat models on them as well as a single desk behind which Shoz Boatswain sits when she isn't busy fixing or building ships.

The Docks

The docks form the western side of Baytide and are primarily wooden docks made for smaller river boats, however there are 4 cut stone docks that can handle keelboats. About 50 feet from the docks themselves are a mix of shops and warehouses that are available for rent. Most warehouses offer at least 10 rooms worth of sleeping quarters.

Dying Tiger Manner

This fortified mansion stands at the edge of Baytide, and is clearly a newer construction. The outer wall is made of a thick, steel fence that seems unaffected by the elements with an inner wall made of dwarven cut stone, which houses most of the actual defenses. The mansion itself is made from 18 trees that have been cut into posts with plastered and painted wood planks forming the red and yellow walls of the mansion.

The inside of the mansion is made primarily with wood planks with carpeting on the floors. The front door leads to a mud room which connects to a easy to clean meeting room and a heavily decorated sitting room with only enough seating for 6 people. The only other notable room of the mansion is the heavily guarded armory, which few people outside of the Order of the Red Sun get to see.

The armory is built into the basement of the manner and is locked via a vault door. Within the armory are 6 magic weapons, only 1 of which Merhiss owns. His magic weapon is a +2 burning spear named Ragethorn.

Behind the manner is a courtyard which houses a wildflower garden and servants' quarters.

Between the inner and outer walls is several public training areas with practice weapons, targets and dummies that are maintained by Lily-Mae Williamson with funds set asside by Merhiss.
