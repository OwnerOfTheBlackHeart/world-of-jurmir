The Black City of Askomoat

  • Ruined city
  • Previous capital
  • Now overrun by the undead
  • Mostly human undead
  • The humans here waged war with the elder races, leading to the current curse the humans are under
  • Patrolled by Lindo clerics who walk among the undead to prevent souls from lingering

Organizations and Monsters

The Keepers of the Black City

The Keepers of the Black City is a collection of Lindites that have taken to maintaining the Black City of Askomoat. They do their best to keep the undead calm and to try to get the spirits of the past to move on, and to remove of any incorporeal undead within the city walls.

Throughout the Black City of Askomoat, the Keepers of the Black City have set up shrines and outposts, with most being headed by a Crypttender, who is in charge of maintaining the protective shrine and performing protective rituals to all lesser keepers to travel about Askomoat without issue.

Notable Locations

The Gates

A pair of plastered brick towers on either side of a brown and dull green stone portcullis, the Gates is the only remaining entrance into Askomoat. Within the Gates is a small "village" containing 34 permanent residents in plastered dirt homes, with the shrine that protects the Gates being placed at the center in a structurally stable, but well worn, stone house that is missing its second floor.

The crypttender of the Gates is Crypttender Zamissur Screaming Cock.

The Hall of the Seven Gods

A massive square stone church believed to be from the time before the human gods, the signs of ancient dwarven craftsmanship are abundant with perfect arches, rectangular doorways and expertly crafted mosaics of each of the seven gods present. The shrines present are of, in order from left to right, Sithrak, Zigies, Durn, Kyranious, Kriotz, Zoltan, and Lindo (which shows no signs of his status as the god of death), with the shrines nearer the center being more prominent. The main temple is well kept, with fine pews filling the nave and a black carpet. In the basement lies 2 rooms with 8 bunks each that house the permanent personnel of the Hall.

The crypttender of the Hall of the Seven Gods is Crypttender Karrissushna Wilting Grass.

The Fick Crypt

A mud brick crypt with a wooden door stands at the center of 17 carriage sized tents with a simple shrine to Lindo placed in the entry way of the crypt. Above the door lies the word "Fick" in a, now unused, cursive style alphabet. The Fick Crypt itself sits in the center of the largest graveyard within Askomoat.

The crypttender of the Fick Crypt is Crypttender Thessan Nearing Sun.

Poole's House

A relatively modern construction within Askomoat, Poole's House is a wattle and daub mansion within a wall made of 7 foot tall wooden planks interspersed with occasional sections of 2 inch think, 45 foot long walls of stone with crude crenellations. The mansion itself is 3 stories tall with small farms growing where gardens would be placed on a typical mansion's lawn. The inside of the house is well furnished, with carpets, rugs, paintings and busts, all of Lindo or of scenes important to the worshipers of Lindo, though a constant gloom fills the air. At the back of the ball room of the mansion lies the well cared for alter of Lindo, made from finely carved bone.

The quarters of Poole's House houses 41 permanent occupants, 10 of which are house servants (a male pantherfolk, female leopardfold, 2 female gnomes, a female half elf, 4 female tieflings and a male tiefling).

The crypttender of Poole's House is Crypttender Dakota Poole.

The Greenflame Crater

Surrounding a fenced off crater, a ring of partially restored homes house the clergy maintaining the alter at the center of the Greenflame Crater. The houses are mostly made of mud brick, though sections have clearly been re-plastered and/or repaired with wood or round stone, and house the 11 residents of the crater. The crater itself is 57 feet in diameter with a set of steep stairs leading to a bone alter at the center, above which floats a ball of unsettling green flame, which is nearly 12 feet in diameter.

Rumors have it that the Greenflame Crater is the physical location where the infertility curse was placed on humanity, though the nature of the Greenflame itself is poorly understood.

The crypttender of the Greenflame Crater is Crypttender Wynvaris Keldeth.

4 Kings' Crypt

Placed at the ruins of the original castle of Askomoat, the 4 Kings' Crypt is the remains of an old tradition of burying the last 4 kings within a temporary crypt until another king dies and the oldest of the buried kings is taken to their family crypt. The current residents of the white and black crypt are unknown to modern scholars, though their corpses remain proudly on display and maintained by the clerics of Lindo. The clerics live in the few structurally sound rooms of the round stone castle, hosting 13 to 27 permanent residents, depending on the number of rooms being actively kept up. The shrine lies in the center of the decayed main hall of the castle, completely exposed to the elements, yet unharmed.

The crypttender of the 4 Kings' Crypt is Crypttender Zegnih the Dark.

King Lange's Crypt

Unlike the many shallow crypts within and around Askomoat, King Lange's Crypt is a small, plastered, mud brick building that leads to an underground complex dedicated to keeping the corpse of King Lange trapped within and undisturbed. The gate to King Lange's Crypt is an almost new steel bar door, locked by an enchanted amazing/superior lock (DC 50). Surrounding King Lange's Crypt is a massive graveyard from the time of King Lange's defeat, which itself is placed around a pile of stone rubble in the vague shape of an ancient castle.
