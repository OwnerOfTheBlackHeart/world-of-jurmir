Cults Yet to Build

Demon

Kugmoxon

Kugmoxon is a balor who is worshiped for his strength and his power. He uses his cultists to constantly feed himself and live in luxury, though he is willing to give up all of it if it would keep him out of hell. Kugmoxon is lazy, but he's always up for a "good old fashioned killing."

Kugmoxon teaches that power is the path to freedom and that anything that grants power makes right.

Ragrunnoth

Ragrunnoth is a glabrezu who is worshiped for his promises of power and wealth. He uses his cultists to create perpetual schemes that slowly drain communities of their wealth and bring about economic ruin.

Ragrunnoth teaches that power and wealth are finite resources that must be taken, as "earning" them takes too long to be meaningful.

Devil

Vano

Vano is a cornugon (horned devil) who was summoned to the Material Plane 100 years ago by an unknown force, however when he was summoned, he ended up locked in a ruined fortress. A few years ago, Vano was able to escape and has begun a personal crusade against the forces of Sithrak. Vano uses his cultists to hunt down and kill Sithraki forces, such as clerics and true drow.

Vano teaches his servants that destroying Sithrak will allow the forces of Hell to march onto the Material Plane and allow them become demons themselves.

Good

Muriel

Muriel is an astral deva (angel) who seeks the destruction of evil. She uses her cultists to relentlessly hunt evil.

Muriel teaches her followers that it is better to destroy evil before it can hard the innocent than to try and protect all who are innocent from evil.

Muriel has a strong dislike of Zarach, and views the archon as being stagnant and unwilling to do what needs to be done.

Zarach

Zarach is an improved hound archon who seeks to protect the innocent from danger. He uses his cultists to protect locations he believes will come under attack by dark forces.

Zarach teaches his followers to protect the innocent over hunting down evil, as actively hunting down evil only serves to leave the innocent vulnerable to the forces of evil.

Zarach dislikes Muriel, believing that her overly aggressive approach to dealing with evil does more harm than good and takes valuable guardians away from those who need them most.

Jeha

Jeha is an avoral (guardinal) who seeks to find a better way to keep the Void locked shut, preferably a method that doesn't require any form of sacrifices. She hopes that by doing this, she can lessen Sithrak's power and influence among mortals and gods, and to be able to rid the world of his creations. Jeha uses her cultists to seek answers on pre-god magics as much as to "surgically remove the evil," as Jeha puts it.

Jeha teaches her followers to focus on their main task, but to remove of evil should it become convenient.

Seros

Seros is a ghaele (eladrin) who seeks to aid the powers of good and throw off the chains of the oppressors. He uses his cultists to find those who are oppressed and aid them in freeing themselves.

Seros teaches his followers that freeing people from the grasp of tyrants and giving them the tools of self defense is the best way to protect against evil.

Baraq

Baraq is a couatl who seeks to turn those who are under the influence of evil over to the side of good. He uses his cultists to serve as examples of positive behavior, to find and report on those who are redeemable, and to offer guidance to those who straddle the line between good and evil.

Baraq teaches his followers to only destroy evil when necessary to protect good and innocence, otherwise, evil is to be taught how to be good.

Followers of the Pearl Cat

The Followers of the Pearl Cat are a cult of charitable workers who worship a pearl statue of a house cat. They believe that if they show generosity to all who need it, when those people no longer need it, they will become generous in turn. The preferred sacrifice of the Followers of the Pearl Cat is small gems, which they believe is eaten in the night when the statue comes to life. They believe that they are only allowed to look upon the statue during the day.

Evil

Yephim, the Puppy of Doom

Yephim is a nessian warhound (hell hound) who seeks the dominance of the forces of Hell and of the Red King. Yephim uses his cult to wear down the will of the institutions that try to enforce goodness and that prevent the King's return.

Yephim teaches his servants that power comes as much from sudden violence as careful, deliberate, and slow action.

Lecha

Lecha is a rakshasa who seeks to slowly tear down the current civilization and replace it with a system she controls. She uses her cultists to infiltrate governments and alter the laws to cause eventual chaos.

Lecha teaches her servants that the problem with the current world is that the existing governments lack the will to make decisive action and would be better served by having Lecha rule them.
