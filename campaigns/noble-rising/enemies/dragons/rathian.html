Description

Typically 57 feet in length and 19 feet at the hip, this wyvern's scaled hide is a dull, muted green while her lower mandible features a long, protruding chin spike. Her upper back and wingtips are covered in a moss-like fur while her clubbed tail has sharp and highly poisonous needles.

Combat

While rathians can fly, their mass makes it energy intensive to maintain powered flight. As such, rathians prefer to fight on the ground, where their strong legs can propel them after their foes, reserving flight for short, near ground bursts of speed and long range gliding. A rathian may also hover for 1d4 rounds, restricting them to only tail attacks and fireballs, but rendering them immune to non-reach melee weapons. While hovering, rathians will use their wingover manuever to face their opponents, which may be followed up with a fireball or a tail attack.

If hunting, rather than defending their nest, rathians are liable to use their snatch feat to grab a medium, or smaller, creature and carry them to the rathian's nest, where they will be eaten.

When a rathian's nest is empty of eggs or chicks, she will have no hesitation to flee should an enemy overpower her, but if eggs or chicks are present in a rathian's nest, she will fight to the bitter end to defend them.
