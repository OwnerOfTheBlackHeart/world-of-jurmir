A Bow for Akkus

Intro

Seeking to gain favor with her god, Nevaeh Byrne has come to Nera's temple with a mutually beneficial proposal: Nera will seek out a bow worthy of Akkus and Nevaeh perform the offering to Akkus.

Enemy CRs

  • CR 5 - 9 (3 large water elementals, 1 large air elemental, 2 adult tojanida, 3 greater barghest)
  • CR 6 - 3 (1 chain devil, 1 lightning bolt trap, 1 belker)
  • CR 7 - 7 (2 pit traps of holding, 2 lillends, 2 huge fire elementals, 1 hellcat)
  • CR 8 - 3 (1 elder xorn, 2 erinyes [devils])
  • CR 9 - 10 (2 greater earth elemental, 1 avoral, 1 dropping ceiling trap, 1 nessian warhound, elder tojanida, 1 incendiary cloud trap, 1 bone devil, 1 wide mouth pit trap, 1 night hag)
  • CR 10 - 1 (1 couatl)
  • CR 11 - 2 (1 elder fire elemental, 1 elder water elemental)
  • CR 13 - 1 (1 ghaele)
  • CR 14 - 1 (1 astral deva [angel])

General Outline

  1. Nera visits her temple
  2. Nevaeh attempts to convince Nera:
    • Nevaeh mentions that this will be a sure fire way to draw Akkus to the temple (possibly mentioning that it will be an offering she can't ignore).
    • If Nera pushes for it, Nevaeh will join her on the quest.
    • If Nera seems uninterested, Nevaeh will offer information on the cult of Cezvoc, specifically about the existence of the Unworded One and the fact that a single cleric seems to be the source of the current spreading of the cult.
  3. Once Nevaeh has convinced Nera, she will reveal the following potential bows to her:
    • Orc Slayer: An ancient elven bow passed down through generations of wars between elven and orcish tribes. Is a +3 bane (orc) composite longbow (14).
      • Located in a small village hidden in Shanta Revar
    • Skyshatter: The exact origins of this bow are unknown, but this bow made of a shaped storm was brought into the Material by Brydi Aspenwish (dick girl), a powerful elven druid, as a gift for a friend of theirs (the exact name of which has been lost to time). +2 shocking burst composite longbow (16). Targets of a crit must make a DC 20 fort save, or be stunned until the end of their next turn.
      • Located somewhere in the swamps of Dhaer'quess's Shal
    • Thalti: A longbow made of black metal, believed to have originated somewhere in Hell. +2 distance seeking composite longbow (20).
      • Located near Kala, Deaavh Hilluk.
    • Yahribel: A longbow seemingly made from undamaged pearl, Yahribel is said to have been wielded by a solar before they met their end to a powerful yuan-ti "hero". Yahribel is said to have been returned to the surface by a mind flayer trading it for safe passage. +2 holy composite longbow (20).
      • Located in one of the towers of the Eight Great Towers.
    • Room 1: This room contains the entrance to the tower, the base of the textured black tower and a stairwell ascending the tower. Scuffs on the floor signal that carpeting and furniture was once housed here, but the room is completely empty. The perfectly patterned granite shows further evidence of looting, as chunks have been chiseled out of the walls.
    • Room 5: Coming up the stairs from the well looted rooms below, a layer of soot covers the final steps and the floor above. A smell not unlike a smokey tavern hangs heavily in the air, while a flickering light far brighter than a candle leaves little hope of an easy climb.
      • Fire elemental, elder
    • Room 6: As one climbs to this room, the sound of a mill with no wheat can be heard. 2 huge masses of stone operate the milling wheel, though they become hostile upon sight of an intruder.
      • 2 pit traps of holding. CR 7; magical; location trigger; repair reset; DC 25 Reflex save avoids; 70 ft. deep (7d6, fall); multiple targets (first target in each of two adjacent 5-ft. squares); Search DC 27; Disable Device DC 18. Market Price: 24,500 gp.
        1. Located on the stairs into the room.
        2. Located within the room.
      • 2 Earth Elementals, Greater
    • Room 11: The sound of heated arguments can be heard from the room above, though it is indistinct and slightly mad. Once a third party enters the room, the two outsiders silence themselves and immediately attack.
      • Avoral
      • Chain Devil
    • Room 14: This room, upon entry, is completely silent. Not even one's own movements make a sound.
      • Dropping Ceiling, activates upon entry, DC 18 to notice before too late: CR 9; mechanical; location trigger; repair reset; ceiling moves down (12d6, crush); multiple targets (all targets in a 10-ft.-by-10-ft. room); never miss; onset delay (1 round); Search DC 20; Disable Device DC 16. Market Price: 12,600 gp.
    • Room 20: Entering into this room, 3 water elementals rinse, wash, then rinse long ruined fabrics while an air elemental dries those that have finished washing.
      • 3 large water elementals
      • 1 large air elemental
    • Room 22: This room is furnished as a small den, with a nice, well upholstered chair and a similarly nice couch, a bookshelf full of entertainment books, from stories of knights and dragons to smutty stories, with a focus on lesbian stories.
      • 2 Lillends
    • Room 23: A strange wind fills the stairway leading up to this room, from which a sound reminiscent of an earthquake arguing with a tornado emanates.
      • Belker
      • Xorn, Elder
      • Lightning Bolt Trap: CR 6; magic device; proximity trigger (alarm); automatic reset; spell effect (lightning bolt, 10th-level wizard, 10d6 electricity, DC 14 Reflex save half damage); Search DC 28; Disable Device DC 28. Cost: 15,000 gp, 1,200 XP.
    • Room 24: An aggressive, agitated barking comes from this room. Within the room is 5 cages, all of which have been broken out of. Four sets of dog-like skeletons line the area around the cages, with only a single nessian warhound left alive.
      • Nessian Warhound
    • Room 26: Entering this room is a strange experience, as one finds themselves completely submerged but perfectly capable of breathing. The denizens of this room can be bought off with food
      • Elder Tojanida
      • 2 Adult Tojanida
    • Room 27: While the rooms of this tower have varied wildly in size, this room is tall in the extreme, the ceiling raising more than 200 feet above the top of the stairs leading to the next room. Near the top is a flat perch, upon which two beautiful, winged women rest. If Elizabeth is willing to part with two 5th level spell slots, she can grant them to the two women, who will then plane shift out of the tower.
      • 2 Erinyes Devils
      • Incendiary Cloud Trap: CR 9; magic device; proximity trigger (alarm); automatic reset; spell effect (incendiary cloud, 15th-level wizard, 4d6/round for 15 rounds, DC 22 Reflex save half damage); Search DC 33; Disable Device DC 33. Cost: 60,000 gp, 4,800 XP.
    • Room 28: Even from below, the agitated arguing coming from this room can be heard. Three distinct voices can be heard, their heated conversation switching seamlessly between goblin, worg and infernal.
      • 3 Greater Barghest
    • Room 33: A noble and powerful looking elven woman stands in this room, her eyes seemingly made purely of glowing radiant energy and her greatsword looking as if it's made of blue flames. The elf introduces herself as Lailah, a ghaele, an eladrin trapped here by the wizard Erik Khan. She implores that she be released from the spell that binds her here, but warns that if one gets too close to the stairs to the next floor, she will be forced to kill them without mercy.

      Remove curse or dispel magic (DC 15).
      • Ghaele
    • Room 34: Compared to other rooms, this room is glorious in it's splendor. The beautiful livery covering the walls and dragon's hoard of fine pillows all lead to a section of room that can be best described as a shrine.
      • Couatl
    • Room 36: This room is oppressively hot and filled with bronze pipes and tanks. Under 2 of the tanks are flames that move as if they are alive, towards any intruder.
      • 2 huge fire elementals
    • Room 37: Connected to the floor below, a massive bronze tank holds what appears to be living water, with a small portion being regularly pulled away. A door with an alchemically reinforced glass window clearly displays the displeasure of the living water.
      • Elder water elemental
    • Room 39: This room is dark, making it difficult for anyone to see, save for a single bright glowing light that can be seen from the stairs leading into the room.
      • Devil, hellcat
      • Bone devil
    • Room 41: This room appears to be a torture chamber, with many insidious devices along the walls. At the front center of the room is a young, elven woman who is chained to the wall.
      • Night Hag
      • Wide-Mouth Pit Trap: CR 9; mechanical; location trigger; manual reset; DC 25 Reflex save avoids; 100 ft. deep (10d6, fall); multiple targets (all targets within a 10-ft.-by-10-ft. area); Search DC 25; Disable Device DC 25. Market Price: 40,500 gp.
    • Room 42: This room appears to be in immaculate condition, with a circle of runes drawn on the floor between the stairs from below and the stairs to the next floor. The angel, Pamyel, appears from the circle and pleads for the party to leave the tower, as he wishes to stop being forced into pointless violence. The moment the party enters the floor, Pamyel will attack without hesitation.
      • Angel, Astral Deva, possibly up to 3 health bars
      • Consider adding a minor summon he can call upon
    • Room 43: The last fully intact room in the tower, this room is filled with collected treasures organized in well kept display cases, though the nicest display case, a piece of marble lined with silver and gold, holds Yahribel, a beautiful bow made of undamaged pearl.
