Acquiring the Lodestone

Intro

After visiting town of Chillbreak and interacting with Xiiserra Rusting Pan, the party may be asked to retrieve a lodestone from a nearby ruin.

Enemy CRs

General Outline

  1. Xiiserra asks the party to help her.
  2. Xiiserra tells the party:
    • The artifact she's after is called a lodestone, and is the only one she's heard of that can be transported.
    • Lodestones can occasionally be found in the wild, though their properties are mostly unknown because they "run" away.
    • She will claim that she was hired to retrieve the lodestone for Holy College of the Deciphered Mystery. Bluff DC 25, Forgery DC 25.
    • If asked, she will present a slightly rusted and beat up iron slayer's tag.
    • The particular ruins she's after are only about 3 miles from Chillbreak, but are quite well hidden.
  3. Xiiserra leads the party to the ruins of the Dangerous Object Storage facility.
    • Room 1: This large room has tiled stone floors and smooth stone walls. The perfect spacing and relative smoothness, considering the ruin's apparent age, indicate that magic was used to either clean up the construction or to make the room. The floor is covered in splotches of mud, blood and some strange goop.
      Enemies:
      • 6 Skum Warriors
      • 2 Skum Heavy Warriors
    • Room 2: This room is almost entirely covered in darkness and spiderwebs, with several dead, and naked, skum only lightly cocooned.
      Enemies:
      • 2 driders
    • Room 3: The floor of this room is covered in stone rubble. When the party enters, a voice screams into their heads "A CHALLENGER APPROACHES?" If the party replies "no," the black pudding will do nothing.
      Enemies:
      • Elder black pudding
    • Room 4: This room appears to be some kind of observation room. Solid glass walls, which have been enchanted to be as strong as iron, look into rooms 2 and 6. Decaying wooden benches face each room. Stony rubble lines the floor.
      • Large earth elemental
    • Room 5:This room appears to be some kind of observation room. Solid glass walls, which have been enchanted to be as strong as iron, look into rooms 5 and 7. Decaying wooden benches face each room. Stony rubble lines the floor. 3 chests and a spare cloak have been left in this room.
      • 3 mimics
      • 1 cloakers
    • Room 6: This room has sacks of half rotten food and a wash basin filled with spoiled water, as well as a 20 foot deep chute, where a gelatinous cube rests at the bottom.
    • Room 7: This room is unremarkable except for the excessive number of skeletons and the horde of gibbering mouthers
      • 7 gibbering mouthers
    • Room 8: This room is filled with 9 sets of bunk beds, all of which are moldy and wet. The room is notably more humid than the rest of the ruin.
      Enemies:
      • 9 Skum Warriors
      • 3 Skum Heavy Warriors
    • Room 9: This room appears to be some form of hallway, with several doors on one side.
      • 2 destrachan
    • Room 10: This room appears to be some kind of observation room. Solid glass walls, which have been enchanted to be as strong as iron, look into rooms 12, 14, and 16. Decaying wooden chairs face each room.
    • Room 11: This room appears to be some kind of observation room. Solid glass walls, which have been enchanted to be as strong as iron, look into rooms 13, 15, and 17. Decaying wooden chairs face each room.
    • Room 12: In the center of this room lies an obsidian altar with a sign, written in celestial, that says "Don't Touch". If a character touches the altar, a random evil god's avatar is summoned, though if the character thinks of a specific evil god, the avatar of that god is summoned. Instructions on the use of the altar are written on it in infernal (on the side facing room 10).
      If Elizabeth touches it, have it summon Ilvith.
    • Room 13: In the center of this room is a brass altar with a sign, written in celestial, that says "Don't Touch". If a character touches the altar, the altar may summon an efreeti, elder fire element, or noble salamander. The summoned will demand something to burn, but will otherwise be initially friendly. If the character thinks of a specific creature or individual from the plane of fire, the altar will instead summon what the character thought of. Instructions on the use of the altar are written on in it in ignan (on the side facing room room 11).
    • Room 14: The only object in this room is a finely made golden throne, even if the style is a few centuries out of date. If character sits in the chair, their alignment shifts 1 stage closer to CE, first on the good evil scale, then the lawful chaotic scale. If the character is already CE, then the throne grants the character a single dark wish (the wish must be distinctly evil, such as murder or a wish for dark magic). The chair applies its effects upon sitting down and only grants 1 wish to any specific individual.
    • Room 15: This room contains a simple wooden table with an unremarkable wooden chest on it. No metal is present on the chest or table, with fire-hardened being used where metal normally would be. Compared to other rooms, this one remains unmolested, with only the layer of dust on the floor giving away the room's age. Within the chest is the lodestone, as well as the Lodestone Report.
    • Room 16: In this room rests a large pool covered in some kind of goopy film (aboleth mucus). Signs of skum activity are dotted across this room. The aboleth mage's name is Sollmulach and he is generally friendly, unless provoked. He was born in the facility, but refuses to answer the question directly.
      Enemies:
      • Aboleth mage
    • Room 17: The exact purpose of this room has been lost to time, however, a 15 diameter runic circle sits at the center of the room. The circle no longer registers any magic, though one of the runes of the circle is missing.
      • 1 destrachan
      • 4 otyughs
      • 2 Skum Heavy Warriors
