Zigvail is a small town of aberromancers built into the ruins of the long lost buildings of Zigvail. The central building is an ancient skyscraper in which the administrative facilities and church to the Red King with smaller two to five story buildings surrounding the skyscraper in which most of the town live. Around the town is not a great wall, instead a series of magical devices that ward off uncommanded aberrations.

The buildings are made of ancient concrete, glass, and steel with patches made from fitted bricks and carved, fitted concrete chunks. Unlike the cold gray buildings that make up most of the Deadlands, the buildings of Zigval are brightly painted and seemingly full of life.

Organizations and Monsters

The Pearl Saints

A group of powerful aberromancers, the Pearl Saints provide the people of Zigvail with both spiritual guidance and protection. Worshipping the Red King and believing that it is their mandate to build up the Red King's army in preparation for the return of the Primordials (which they believe the current gods incapable of holding). To do this, they use the people of Zigvail to serve as both aberration incubators and as soldiers, requiring all women born in Zigvail to give birth to two aberrations before they are considered adults and citizens, while men must serve in the military for at least two years for the same rights.

The Pearl Saints maintain a strick law code and keep order like a military, though they are few in comparison to the citizens and the citizenry have a less strict culture which is far laxer concerning the law... save those in active service, who other citizens act properly around.

The Pearl Saints are lead by Grand Saint Reshef, with Kadlin Geirdottir serving as the High Paladin. The Pearl Saints operate out of the Tower of the Saints.

Ruin Raiders

The Ruin Raiders are the closest to adventurers that the people of Zigvail have, occasionally leaving Zigvail to delve into the surrounding ruins to gather materials, parts and scavenge food. Their expeditions outside Zigvail also provide scouting services for the Pearl Saints.

The Ruin Raiders operate out of Ruins' Hall.

Notable Locations

The Tower of the Saints

The Tower of the Saints is a skyscraper with 17 stories of enclosed floors remaining and a 18th roof floor, along with a 6 story basement garage filled with Everburning Torches that is setup to stable horses and wagons of medium size or smaller. The outer walls of the Tower are painted crimson and pearl. The front door is a set of four double doors, all made of clear glass, with a statue of a different human warrior before each door. To the right of the front door is a cement pad with a large, tall overhang that provides a place for horses and wagons to be parked, as well as the entrance to the basement garage.

The three floors of the Tower of the Saints are dedicated to the Church of Churnobog, save a small guard office on the first floor. The fourth floor holds the administrative offices, and the remaining 12 stories are used by the Pearl Saints for everything from housing and mess halls to ritual rooms and libraries.

The first floor of the Church of Churnobog is a grand entrance flanked by statues of human warriors, priests and various aberrations, leading to a hallway with several classrooms and a small town guard office, with a stairway that leads to the main sanctuary on the second and third floor. The sanctuary itself takes up two floors with a pair of statues of Churnobog that look like they are holding up the rest of the building alongside colored glass murals of the War Against the Gods. The opposite end of the sanctuary from the entry stairs is the altar, chancel, and pulpit, raised high so the people sitting in the pews filling the nave can hear the speaker. The ceiling is arched and painted with a mural depicting the Pearl Saints saving the people of Zigvail from a horde of monsters.

Grand Saint Reshef, Guard Captain Zir Yah, and High Paladin Kadlin Geirdottir have their offices in the Tower of the Saints.

Ruins' Hall

Ruins' Hall is a 5 story tall skyscraper ruin with a recently renovated leather and bone door. The outer walls are painted orange and blue with 6 heroes of the Raiders painted onto the exterior walls depicting their great dead. Inside, the first three stories are a single floor with an open common area and quest counter in the immediate entrance with a second floor balcony holding the bar that holds the administrative offices below the balcony. The 4th floor serves as a hospital for Raiders injured in the line of duty while the 5th floor holds rooms that are rented out to Raiders who need them. The "roof" is setup as a garden area and running track for the Raiders to relax and exercise in.

The Ruin Raiders operate out of Ruins' Hall. Gronga and Oma can be frequently found hanging around Ruins' Hall.

The Terraces

The Terraces are a series of smaller ruins of skyscrapers that have had their exposed sections built into clusters of flat structures covered in dirt, water, and crops that resemble terrace farms. Due to the hostile environment of the Deadlands, the Terraces are constantly worked by a large number of people. The floors below the individual terraces are used for storage.

Most of the buildings that make up the Terraces are painted green and blue, though a few are painted red or yellow.

Springs and Gears

Springs and Gears is a 4 story building with a clear smithy on the roof and a sign depicting a gear made from bronze hanging over the front door. The outside walls are painted bronze and patina green. A handful of random scrap statues are placed in front of Springs and Gears, occasionally changing as new ones are made and old ones are melted down into scrap.

The first floor of Springs and Gears is the administrative floor with the front desk and offices, while the remaining 3 floors are dedicated to working with bronze, alchemical scrap and clockwork scrap. The roof has 6 different smelters and smithies, each designed for and kept at different temperatures to work with different metals.

Bronze equipment can be purchased from Springs and Gears. Certain clockwork devices can be commissioned at Springs and Gears, but the commissioner is required to bring enough clockwork scrap for the piece to be made.

Borte currently serves as the master smith of Springs and Gears.

Kitten's Cradle

The Kitten's Cradle is a 3 story building that is not a skyscraper, but a new construction made from fitted bricks with the four corners being made from carved chunks of concrete, all painted to look like the sky during sunset. The first floor is the entrance, kitchen and a few of the cheaper rooms. The second floor holds the majority of the rooms and even includes a shared shower and bath room with running, sun-heated water. The third floor is split into two suites that can each hold up to 10 people and include private baths and running, sun-heated water. The roof is setup as a sitting area for guests to use.

About half of the rooms on the second floor are apartments.

The Kitten's Cradle is owned and operated by Kannan.
