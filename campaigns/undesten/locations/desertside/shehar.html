Built not far off the fork of the Bloodfloat River, Shehar is a city with a thick plastered brick wall, inviting docks and well made wooden buildings.

Organizations and Monsters

Adventurers' League

Supporting the majority of Undesten with monster hunting and ruin raiding, the Adventurers' League is an organization that was created to support, organize and monitor adventurers within Undesten. Being that Shehar is the region's capital and that the Shah's Palace, and the court within, are in Shehar, the Adventurers' League has setup their headquarters within the city.

Notable Locations

Shah's Palace

The Shah's Palace is a massive siheyuan style building with three plastered stone stories with five more stories made of wood, all painted with beautiful murals of naturalistic scenes, both of the desert and swamps. The inside of the palace is beautiful and opulent, filled with statues and artistically carved columns, large paintings, gilded walls and plentiful attractive servants. The outside of the palace is a large and deep garden with a gaudy marble fountain, countless carefully managed flowers and hedge mazes containing benches where visitors and aristocrats can sit and talk.

The Shah's Palace is home to Shah Asal Abdi.

Bastard's Bath

The Bastard's Bath is a large building made of plastered brick and painted in vibrant colors with a garish sign painted in pastels. The front doors lead immediately into an open room with a secretary's desk and couches, where bathers hang about waiting to be hired. The door to the left leads to the baths, made from carved stone with heating fires burning beneath, while the right door leads to a number of rooms rentable by the hour, each containing a nice bed, a sink with clean water in it and a chamberpot.

The Bastard's Bath is both a bathhouse and a whorehouse, combined into one. They offer bathers/whores in all shapes and sizes.

Market of Heroes

A small market with a silver engraved sign, the market of heroes is made up of a single well adorned stage with well made, and painted, steel cage. The guards of the Market of Heroes are clearly well payed mercenaries and the single notary is equally well adorned. What few people hang around the Market of Heroes are adventuring types and wealthy slavemasters.

The Market of Heroes only sells player classed characters, sold as pre-trained adventures and sold to adventurer guilds and parties looking for an easy replacement or to supplement a lack of interest in their group.

Market of Flesh

Based around a meat market where fish, crocodile and various other animal meats are sold, the real value to outsiders and merchants is the fact that the Market of Flesh also sells normal slaves. While lacking the raised stands that one would see in a dedicated slave market, slavemasters and fishermen alike are welcome to rent out the stalls of the market to sell their flesh to passers by.

The MEGAplex

The MEGAplex is a massive structure made of massive sandstone bricks covered in facades from marble to bamboo that houses the headquarters of the Adventurers' League as well as a interior market provides nearly everything an adventurer could conceivably need, a food court with a surprising variety of options and rentable living quarters.

The headquarters of the Adventurers' League takes up nearly a quarter of the MEGAplex with its many offices, library, training facilities and vault. Sections of the headquarters meant for dealings with clients use marble and gold facades with many well made murals, while those for guild members are covered in a comfortable, if plain, bamboo facade.

The market is decorated in painted murals and silver inlays on top of plaster over the sandstone walls, and contains a multitude of shops open to both the public and Adventurers' League members. Some of the shops of note include a bank that offers Adventurers' League members coin changing at rates of 5% (instead of the normal 10%), a smithy, a general goods store, a fletcher, a tailor, a stablemaster (for selling animals kept in the MEGAplex's stables), and more.

The food court is relatively austere compared to the rest of the MEGAplex with the sandstone walls left bare and simple, comfortable tables laid around the food court. Human, elven, dwarven, catfolk and even ratfolk cuisine can all be found about the food court, with Adventurers' League members getting half off on fresh meals. A bar is also present, selling everything from dwarven whiskey to imported elven wines and meads from Iirie, but it offers no discounts.

The inn included in the MEGAplex is available to the public, though Adventurers' League members have priority for rooms and get half off their stays. The inn has individual rooms (good) and several suites of varying sizes and configurations available, and a small number of whores that offer their services to lonely adventurers and merchants.
