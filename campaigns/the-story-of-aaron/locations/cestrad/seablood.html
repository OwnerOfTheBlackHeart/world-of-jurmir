The City of Seablood

The city of Seablood is a port city and is run by a group of Sithrak worshipping mercenaries. A branch of the Cult of the Red King has also been gaining power.

Settlement Information

Town Size: Small City
Population: Approximately 10,000 people
Gold Limit: 15,000 gp
Power Center(s) 2 nonstandard
Power Center Alignment Lawful evil, chaotic evil
Guard Captain Power Highest level fighter
Guard Count 200 guards (100 shift 1, 60 shift 2, 40 shift 3), 500 militiamen
Demographics Type Integrated
Political System Dictatorship

Organizations and Monsters

The Judges

Sithrak worshipping mercenaries run by Dinzar Grifter who control the city of Seablood. They have a bounty out for Red King cultists (100gp for a dead cultist, 250gp for a live cultist) that requires proof of the person being a cultist.

Notable Peoples

Dinzar Grifter

Drow-half-gnoll leader of the Judges and son of Drust Grifter. Cleric of Sithrak. Possibly brought back through time by Lagos in order to prevent the Red King from rising.

Edwin Coxe

Elf male, 157 years old. 5', 106 lbs. Lightly tanned skin with black hair and midnight blue eyes. He wears a simple green shirt and pants. Carpenter. Father of Kendra Coxe, a kidnapped half-elf.

Kendra Coxe

Half-elf female, 22 years old. 4' 9", 104 lbs. Pale skin with dark brown hair and midnight blue eyes. Alchemist who, when free, wears a simple set of silk clothes with a heavy leather apron.

Skyla Kennarde

Halfling female, 55 years old, 3' 1", 30 lbs. Skyla has pale skin with a ruddy complexion and straight, black hair that reaches her butt, and big eyes the color of almonds. She wears studded leather armor beneath a bright yellow dress and wears 4 necklaces and 12 rings, each with a different type of gem in them.

Before settling down in Seablood, Skyla adventured around as a rogue.

Lane Tatume

Human male, 63 years old, 5' 10", 168 lbs. Lane has light black skin with curly brown hair that is kept relatively short and dark brown eyes. Lane wears sturdy leather clothes.

Nell Glasse

Human female, 22 years old, 5', 134 lbs. Nell is chubby with tanned skin, wheat colored hair that goes down to her shoulders, and pale blue eyes. She wears basic furs and owns Castle of Glasse.

Nell is pregnant with 3 to 4 of Aaron's children, which will be born on Durnio 7th, 1489.

Nell was once a cultist of the Red King. She used her connections, while still in the cult, in order to get her shop up and running quickly. However, she cut herself off from the cult when they tried to get her to become a Red Queen.

Lorna Wesleye

Human masculine herm, 48 years old, 5' 10", 168 lbs. Lorna is scholarly pale with strawberry blonde hair that is kept in a short ponytail and deep green eyes that are reminiscent of an elf's. He wears a gray set of wool wizard robes and a pointy hat.

Lorna runs the Tower of Trinkets. He was given a female name because his parents didn't know if he was going to be masculine or feminine, but he prefers to go by Wes.

Notable Locations

The Castle

Large castle at the center of town. Built out of a strange black stone and is visible anywhere in the city. The inside of the castle is constantly busy with slaves, scribes, and members of the Judges going to and from the maze of hallways and rooms that make up the interior of the castle.

The castle's top floor is a Hall of Heroes, prominently showing Drust Grifter and his small army of slaves. The Hall of Heroes leads to the main worship chamber which holds a 12 foot tall statue of Sithrak along with 3 skeleton embers on either side of the statue.

Public Records Building (PRB)

Throughout the city of Seablood there are 16 different PRBs, each built exactly the same. Each PRB has 6 lanes which each house a single worker who sells law books, hands out updates, and handles the paperwork for ownership, claims, and public records requests. If a PRB can't handle a request, they can usually route a person to the correct place for the request.

PRBs are built of wood plank with dark red roofs and Sithrak skulls beside the door. The inside is a single room that can handle about 35 occupants with two statues of skeletons with war scythes. A single worker stands outside the lanes, handling the routing of customers, either sending them to an open lane or giving them a number. Above each lane, which is a counter with bars and a metal bin for moving items between the sides of the lane, is a set of 3 metal dials that rotate to form the number that the lane is ready to handle.

The Unjudged Sin

A common adventurer tavern run by ex-adventurer Skyla Kennarde. The main bar area is relatively small with only a few round tables, but planning and party rooms are available for rent as are rooms on the second floor. The bottom of the building is made from round stone and the floor is dirt, but the second floor is made from brick and iron.

Dwarven fire whiskey is always kept in stock and may be purchased for 75 gp per bottle. Several drinks made by Underdark Breweries are also available: Absinthe (110 gp per bottle), Spider's Silk (pua with extra flavor, 150 gp per bottle), and Taste of the Underdark (mushroom wine, 80 gp per bottle).

Lane's Lane

A simple dug in, brick building with an open main room filled with various general goods from food to animal supplies. A counter is near the door behind which the owner, Lane Tatume, stands. Lane's Lane doesn't hold gear specific to adventuring.

Castle of Glasse

A single story wattle and daub building made to look like a small castle, Castle of Glasse has a main room inside that has several shelves with everything from 10 foot poles to alchemist fires and acid along with a cob counter behind which Nell Glasse normally works.

Tower of Trinkets

A simple, round cob tower that is approximately 40 feet in diameter and 4 stories tall. The main sales area has several display cases with various trinkets and magic items, all of which are secured with both mundane and arcane protections. The second story contains a proper enchanting lab that, for 500gp rent a day, allows an enchanter to enchant an item at 3 times the normal rate. The third floor is a library filled with various tomes and notebooks covering a variety of arcane topics and costs 50gp a day to rent. The fourth floor is a room for practicing magic that has been enchanted to limit the effects of magic that could potentially harm the caster or the building and is open for public use.

The Tower of Trinkets is owned by Lorna Wesleye, who just seems to appear when you need him.
