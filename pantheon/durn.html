Durn, God of Civilization

Alignment: Lawful Good

Domains: Good, Healing, Protection, Law

Portfolio: Civilization, Law, Healing, Protection

Symbol: A hammer placed on scythe

Colors: Pearl and gold

Durn, the god of civilization, is generally known as the god of law-bound species (such as humans, halflings, and dwarves). He is commonly worshipped by good nobility and guardsmen. He is also regarded as the king of the gods. During the War of the Red King, he created the halflings.

Durn is the brother of Sithrak, the Cruel Judge. After he came to realize to how cruel his brother was, he exiled Sithrak to Hell to become the judge and warden of the prison of vile souls.

While Durn is not a god of commerce or money, it isn't uncommon for his clergy to handle the production and tracking of currency within civilizations that lack other institutions to do so.

Mortal Relations

While many races worship Durn, dwarves and halflings are his truest servants. Humans, with their inherently varied nature, worship Durn only when he appeals to them. Elves, on the other hand, have an odd relationship with Durn, often loving his gifts and his meaning, but often find his worship too stringent. Though, in areas where elves are more lawful, they flock quickly to Durn for his wisdom and guidance on how to rule.
