Hell, The Eternal Prison

Description

Hell appears to exist within a cavern miles high with a floor of pure, raging flame, with landmasses of ashy and charred stone floating about and storms of thick, black, choking smoke. Throughout Hell are seven "pillars," which all points in Hell are equidistant to, requiring only a single thought and step in order to arrive at any of the seven pillars of Hell. At the dead center of Hell rests a seemingly bottomless pit, called Derin Ates Deligi.

The landmasses can range from the size of a house to that of continents and may be barren flat rock, settlements of cowering souls seeking to avoid the gaze of Sithrak, great prisons run by the emberred, or even colonies of devils and demons.

When not acted upon, the landmasses of Hell float about in orderly, predetermined patterns, never colliding or nearing each other in a manner that almost seems like a dance. When a creature is on a landmass and possesses the knowledge of how to do so, they may command the landmass to move across the Sea of Flame, though any creature capable of controlling the landmass who possesses more hit dice may override their "order."

Important Locations

The Tower of Judgement

Carved out of one of the pillars of Hell and floating over Derin Ates Deligi, the Tower of Judgement is where souls go in Hell to be judged. Filling the tower are rooms filled with embers in service to Sithrak as well as a seemingly endless spiralling, staired hallway that leads to the open top of the tower, which is known as The Court of Final Judgement.

The Court of Final Judgement consists of little more than a small entrance coming up from the floor, a flat, clear area where those being judged stand, and the massive throne of Sithrak himself. What the throne is made is unclear, due to the thick char that covers it, though it's shape is that of hard rectangles made in the vague shape of a throne.

The "island" that the Tower of Judgement is on sits almost 100 feet above the rest of the landmasses, held to the edges of Derin Ates Deligi by massive chains and hooks.

Derin Ates Deligi

Roughly translating to "Deep Fire Hole," Derin Ates Deligi is the pit at the center of Hell, directly below the Tower of Judgement. The pit itself is almost circular and is large enough to swallow an entire continent, with countless ash-stone fortifications place around the ring and guarded by every type of denizen in Hell, some of which guard the hooked chains that keep Tower of Judgement where it is above Derin Ates Deligi. Fire pours like liquid from the rim of the pit down into its depths.

Only Sithrak and the guards of Derin Ates Deligi know what truly lies below, though the fact that the forces that guard Derin Ates Deligi do so without defection or conflict, scholars believe that it must be something that either threatens all existence or some great, but sinful, good.

Yangin Evi

Formed around one of the seven pillars of Hell, Yangin Evi is the largest continent in Hell and is home primarily to devils. Countless fortified settlements dot Yangin Evi, where devils trade knowledge, contracts, souls and goods while doing their best to avoid the gaze of Sithrak. The government(s) of Yangin Evi, while constantly shifting as arch-devils compete for power, are well organized and militaristic.

The "capital" of Yangin Evi is Savas Sehri, the fortified city at the base of the pillar, garrisoned with countless devils ready to fight any forces of Sithrak that dare tread on Yangin Evi.

Gunah Yeri

A constantly shifting continental mass primarily populated by demons, Gunah Yeri is considered to be one of the most dangerous places in Hell for mortals to travel to. Due to the fact that Sithrak regularly sends forces to invade Gunah Yeri or cull its demon population, the pillar that resides on the continent is usually kept near its edge.

The settlements of Gunah Yeri appear and disappear sporadically, with demons gathering for odd, and often random seeming, reasons, then disappearing out into the wilds of Gunah Yeri as soon as the reason they gathered is no longer there.

Devils

Believed to be the original denizens of Hell, the devils of Hell are constantly looking for ways to dethrone and unbind Sithrak. Despite this, the fact that devils are masters of getting others to sin while avoiding sin themselves, or using contracts and other agreements to place the sin of their actions on another, most devils can safely exist around the Hateful Judge himself.

The History of Hell

Before Sithrak claimed the plane of Hell, it was little more than a seemly endless cavern with a floor of liquid fire and the occasional passing devil, who hunted for souls that they could feed to the fires of Hell. Many believed that the old god Zoltar had a residence in Hell, though there is currently no way to prove, or disprove, that idea.

When the Red King began to lose in his war against Kyranious and the new gods, he managed to hire a small legion of devils to delay the advancing armies of his enemies. How he was able to gather so many devils at once, or what he exchanged for their service, is unknown even to this day.

After the defeat of the Red King and Zoltar, Hell had a period of rest before Sithrak moved in and began reshaping Hell to his whims. It is around this time that devil populations began to soar, suggesting some form of response to Sithrak's presence. Many planar historians argue as to whether the rise in devil populations was a result of the new flow of vile souls that Sithrak brought with him or are evidence that Hell has an intelligence all its own that controls the devils and their numbers.

A second mass increase in the devil population happened when Sithrak was done with his changes to Hell and bound the plane to himself by planting the Spikes of Absolute Control into his eyes. The cause of the second spike is also unknown, though the leading theories are that the spike was due to Sithrak's power and the energies of Hell mixing, that Hell once again proved itself intelligent, or that when he bound himself to the plane, Sithrak brought even more vile souls into Hell.

When and how Derin Ates Deligi was created is still a mystery, as well as what lies at the bottom.
