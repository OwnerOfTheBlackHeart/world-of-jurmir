The Guild Management System

The guild Management system provided here is meant to handle allowing players to create and manage guilds while they still continue having the ability to adventure.

The Party

The smallest deployable unit of a guild is the party, a group of 2 to 10 adventurers, which is capable of taking on missions for the guild. The party is made up of its level and its members. Parties are typically assembled on the acceptance of a mission or the identification of a guild task.

A party's level represents how capable the party is. A party's level is equal to the average level of the party's members.

A party's members is the number of members in the party. Depending on the mission type, having a higher number of party members could be benificial or it could be counter productive.

When building a party, pay attention to the types of members being chosen. Certain events can be reduced or negated by having certain types of characters in a party.

Guild Members

A guild member represents a single adventurer working for the guild. They are the building blocks from which parties are assembled. They are the strength, the heart, the blood, and the soul of a guild. Each guild member is tracked as a level, experience score, and type.

A guild member's level and experience are intertwined. The level represents the capacity of the individual guild member and the experience how far along to their next level they are. Guild members use the same experience chart as normal characters.

A guild member's type represents what role the guild member plays. Combat members are focused and destroying enemies and surviving lethal blows. Stealth members focus on remaining unseen. Social members understand complex social structures and manipulate their way through them with ease. Divine members offer the blessings of the divines to a guild and provide healing as well as knowledge on religion and cults. Arcane members are in tune with the magic drawn from study or blood, and tend to be good at research. Natural members are one with nature, excelling at navigation, tracking, and surviving on their own.

Recruiting Guild Members

A fresh faced guild member (level 1, 0 experience) can be recruited for 200gp. When a guild member is recruited in this manner, what type they are is randomly determined. For 300gp, you can recruit a level 1 character and choose what type they are.

Higher level members may be recruited through roleplay or may be found during adventures. If the characters can successful recruit the higher level member, they may be added by the GM with an appropriate level and type.

Missions

A mission is the mode by which a guild gains profits and the members gain experience. Every mission has a success DC, required number of successes, travel time, activity time, reward, modifiers, and type.

A mission's success DC is the difficulty check that the party has to roll against in order to gain a success. When a party fails to roll the DC or higher, they make a consequence roll.

A mission's number of success is how many successful rolls are required to successfully complete the mission.

A mission's travel time is how long it takes for the party to get to the mission area and back. This value is added on to by the mission's activity time, which is added once for each mission roll, pass or fail.

A mission's reward is the amount of gold and the amount of experience earned upon successfully completing the mission. The gold is the amount of gold pieces that the guild receives, while the experience is the amount of experience to be distributed among any guild members that participated in the mission.

A mission's modifiers are the modifiers to the success roll based on certain effects of the party sent on it, such as including an arcane member or two combat members or even having less than four members in the party.

A mission's type determines the consequence roll chart used when a party fails to match or beat the mission's success DC.

Completing Missions

A mission begins by selecting the party, or parties, that will handle the mission. When half the travel time has passed, the party may begin rolling mission rolls against the mission's success DC, each roll taking the mission's activity time. If a roll is failed, roll on the consequence roll table matching the type of mission.

If the party, or parties, gets a number of successes equal to mission's required successes, then the mission has been completed. Add the second half of the travel time and receive the gold and experience for completion of the mission. The members that worked on the mission are now freed to be added to new parties.

Failing a Mission

Even though adventuring parties are typically considered to be fairly competent, they may still fail. A mission may fail under two conditions: The mission is time sensitive and time has run out or all guild members on the mission have died.

When a mission is failed, the guild gains no reward, but any surviving members gain half experience.
